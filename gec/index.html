Abstract

We explore transformer-based multilingual models — mT5-small and IndicBART — to correct grammatical and semantic errors across five Indian languages. Due to limited annotated data, we develop a synthetic data augmentation pipeline that introduces realistic linguistic errors under ten categories, simulating natural mistakes found in Indic scripts.

Our best submissions achieve competitive GLEU scores, with linguistically grounded augmentation significantly improving correction accuracy in these low-resource settings.

Key Contributions
  • A linguistically informed synthetic error-injection framework with 42 rules across 10 categories for Indic GEC data augmentation
  • Evaluation and comparison of mT5-small and IndicBART — representing the two dominant pretraining paradigms for Indic languages
  • Scaled training data from <1k to 10k+ high-quality parallel pairs per language via controlled error injection
  • Empirical analysis of dataset scaling, training epochs, and cross-lingual transfer effects
Methodology

Each clean sentence from official BHASHA data and supplementary corpora (AI4Bharat IndicCorp v2, Indic Wikipedia) is treated as a gold reference and transformed into a synthetically "incorrect" version via our controlled error injection framework.

Error distribution per sentence: 60% (1 error), 25% (2 errors), 10% (3 errors), 5% (4+ errors). Each sentence generates five synthetic noisy variants — yielding ~10–12k parallel pairs per language after deduplication.

# Input/Output format
Input: "correct this: [HI] <incorrect sentence>"
Output: "<correct sentence>"

# Training config
LR: 5e-5 (mT5) | 3e-5 (IndicBART)
Batch: 16–32 · Epochs: 10–15
Early stopping: on dev GLEU score
Synthetic Error Categories (10 classes · 42 rules)
Spelling
Non-dictionary matra swaps, consonant substitution; dictionary homophones/misspellings
Tense
Morphological inflection mutations for wrong tense forms
Person / Number / Gender
Subject-verb disagreement in person, number, gender
Case
Wrong postposition (ने→को, को→में, etc.)
Parts-of-Speech
Incorrect word category usage
Missing / Extra
Omission or duplication of postpositions and adverbs
Punctuation
Removal or wrong placement of । / ? / ,
Semantic
Semantically incorrect postposition or adverb choice

Future Directions
  • Incorporate real learner corpora to better reflect diversity and complexity of actual errors
  • Test language-specific pretrained models (BanglaT5, ByT5-based) as stronger baselines
  • Extend augmentation pipeline to more Indic languages
  • Human evaluation of fluency, meaning preservation, and practical usability
  • Advanced cross-lingual and few-shot approaches for ultra-low-resource settings
